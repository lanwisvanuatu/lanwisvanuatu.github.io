Before the Mota Lava Bible app can be released to everyone on Google Play, Google asks us to have a group of people test it first. We are looking for volunteers to try the app on their Android phone and tell us if anything does not work. Thank you for helping!

Plis traem ridim mo lisin long sam japta. Sipos wan samting i no wok gud, i luk nogud, no i no klia, plis sanem wan imel long vanuatusmith@gmail.com.

Plis yu livim ap ia i stap long fon blong yu long 2 manis. Hemi naf taem blong finisim test ia.

What testers do

  • Install the app on your Android phone and use it — read and listen to some Scripture.
  • Keep the app installed for about two weeks (Google requires this for the test).
  • Tell us if you find any problems, or anything that is confusing or does not look right.

What you need

  • An Android phone or tablet.
  • A Google (Gmail) account signed in on that device.
  • A little internet access to download the app and join the group.
Join the testing group

How to join, step by step

  1. Join the testing group

    Tap Join the testing group above. Please join with the same Gmail address you use on your phone — this is how Google knows to offer you the app. If you use a different account, the test will not appear.

  2. Become a tester on Google Play

    After you have joined the group, open this link on your phone and tap “Become a tester”:

    play.google.com/apps/testing/org.sil.vanuatu.motalava.sab

  3. Install from Google Play

    On the same page, tap the link to download it on Google Play, then install the Mota Lava Bible app like any other app.

  4. Use it, then fill in the checklist

    As you try the app, please fill in our short tester checklist. It walks you through the things to check and takes about 10 minutes:

    Fill in the tester checklist

    If you find a problem, you can also post a comment in the testing group or email vanuatusmith@gmail.com.

Please don’t uninstall during the test. Google needs testers to keep the app installed for about two weeks before we can release it publicly. If you have a problem, tell us rather than removing it — that helps us the most.

Questions? Post in the testing group or email vanuatusmith@gmail.com.
